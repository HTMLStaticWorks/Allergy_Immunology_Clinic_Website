Allergen Avoidance

Practical tips for reducing exposure to common environmental triggers at home.

  • Dust Mites

    Dust mites thrive in humid environments and shed proteins that trigger year-round allergies.

    5 Proven Removal Ways
    • • Allergen-Proof Bed Covers: Encase mattress & pillows.
    • • Hot Wash Weekly: Use 130°F (54°C) water for bedding.
    • • Low Humidity: Maintain 30%–50% using A/C or dehumidifiers.
    • • HEPA Vacuuming: Vacuum daily & wet mop hard floors.
    • • Steam Cleaning: Deep clean carpets and furniture.
    Symptoms (Mild to Severe)
    • • Sneezing & nasal congestion
    • • Itchy, watery eyes
    • • Facial pressure & chronic coughing
    • • Asthma attacks & chest tightness
    • • Difficulty breathing or wheezing

    Tip: Avoid using attic/window fans as they pull in more outdoor allergens.

  • Pet Dander

    Keep pets out of the bedroom entirely. Use HEPA air purifiers in main living areas.

  • Indoor Mold

    Keep indoor humidity below 50%. Promptly fix any leaks and clean bathroom surfaces regularly.

  • Pollen & Hay Fever

    Seasonal pollen impacts can reduce lung capacity and worsen chronic conditions like Asthma and COPD.

    Respiratory Health Risks

    Pollen causes airway inflammation, leading to increased mucus, wheezing, and higher susceptibility to lung infections.

    • • Outdoor Avoidance: Keep windows closed on high pollen days.
    • • Hygiene: Shower before bed to remove pollen from hair.
    • • Air Purification: Use personal/portable HEPA filters.
    • • Ionic Technology: Consider negative ion cleaners to clear the breathing zone.

  • Insect Sting Allergy

    Reactions to bee, wasp, and fire ant stings can be severe. Learn about venom immunotherapy as a protective measure to reduce future reaction risks.

  • Drug Allergy

    Adverse reactions to medications like penicillin can be complex. We help distinguish true IgE-mediated allergies from common side effects.

    Allergy vs. Side Effect
    • • True Allergy: Hives, facial swelling, wheezing, or anaphylaxis.
    • • Side Effect: Nausea, headache, or mild stomach upset.
    Our Solutions
    • • Drug Challenges: Safe, supervised in-office testing.
    • • Desensitisation: Temporary tolerance building for essential meds.

  • Advanced Asthma Care

    Explore how biologic therapies can offer new hope for individuals with severe, uncontrolled asthma by targeting specific inflammatory pathways.

  • Immunology Insights

    Understand your body's defense system and how primary immunodeficiency disorders are diagnosed and managed through comprehensive immune evaluations.

Medical Glossary

Demystifying common immunological terms.

Anaphylaxis

A severe, potentially life-threatening allergic reaction that can affect multiple organ systems and requires immediate administration of epinephrine.

Histamine

A chemical released by white blood cells into the bloodstream when the immune system is defending against a potential allergen.

IgE (Immunoglobulin E)

Antibodies produced by the immune system. If you have an allergy, your immune system overreacts to an allergen by producing specific IgE antibodies.

Biologic

A medication manufactured in a living system (like a microorganism) that targets very specific pathways of the cellular immune response.

Our Treatment Protocol

We employ a systematic and evidence-based protocol to ensure every patient receives comprehensive and effective care.

1. Comprehensive Assessment

The journey begins with a thorough review of medical records and a detailed patient interview.

2. Advanced Diagnostics

We perform skin, blood, and lung function tests as needed to get a clear diagnostic picture.

3. Tailored Therapeutics

Based on results, a plan is built, incorporating avoidance strategies, medications, or immunotherapy.

4. Education & Follow-Up

We ensure you understand your plan and schedule regular follow-ups to monitor progress and adjust as needed.

Before You Call

To make your appointment process as smooth as possible, please have the following information ready.

Insurance Information

Have your insurance card handy so we can verify your coverage and benefits before your visit.

Referral Status

If your insurance plan requires a referral from your primary care physician, please ensure it has been sent to our office.

Current Medications

Be prepared to list all current medications, especially antihistamines, which may need to be stopped before testing.

Prior Medical Records

If you have seen other allergists, having those records sent to us can prevent duplicate testing and expedite your care.

Your First Visit

Here’s what you can typically expect during your initial consultation with one of our board-certified allergists.

1. Check-in & Vitals

Our front desk will confirm your paperwork, and a nurse will take your vital signs to start your chart.

2. Physician Consultation

You'll have a one-on-one discussion with the allergist to review your history and symptoms in detail.

3. Same-Day Testing

If appropriate, skin testing may be performed during your visit. Results are available in about 15 minutes.

4. Plan & Check-out

The doctor will discuss findings, outline a treatment plan, and you'll schedule your follow-up at the front desk.

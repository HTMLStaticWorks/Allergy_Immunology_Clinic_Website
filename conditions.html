Conditions We Treat

Precise diagnosis and targeted treatments for deep-rooted allergies.

Respiratory Skin Conditions Food & Drug Insect & Environment Immune Deficiency

Respiratory Allergies

Asthma

Chronic inflammation of the airways. Learn about advanced biologic therapies for severe cases.

PFT Testing

Hay Fever (Allergic Rhinitis)

An allergic response causing itchy, watery eyes, sneezing, and other similar symptoms commonly triggered by pollen, pet dander, or mold.

Immunotherapy

Sinusitis

Chronic swelling of the sinuses leading to recurrent infections, facial pain, and severe congestion. Often linked to underlying, untreated environmental allergies.

Skin Conditions

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Dry, red, itchy skin patches. Often related to food allergies in infants or underlying immune dysregulation.

Hives (Urticaria)

Raised, itchy welts that can appear suddenly. Can be acute (due to food/drugs) or chronic (autoimmune).

Contact Dermatitis

Localized rash resulting from direct contact with substances like nickel, latex, harsh detergents, or poison ivy. Diagnosed via Patch Testing.

Food & Drug Allergies

Nut & Dairy Allergies

Severe systemic reactions including anaphylaxis. We offer precise diagnosis and OIT (Oral Immunotherapy) for desensitization.

Penicillin Testing

90% of patients diagnosed with a penicillin allergy are actually not allergic. We offer safe, in-office testing to safely "de-label" patients.

Insect & Environmental Allergies

We diagnose and provide venom immunotherapy (allergy shots) for those with life-threatening reactions to bee, wasp, hornet, yellow jacket, and fire ant stings. Additionally, we provide tailored avoidance strategies and treatments for indoor environmental triggers like dust mites, pet dander, and cockroaches.

Immune Deficiency

If you or your child suffers from unusually frequent, severe, or long-lasting infections, it may indicate an underlying Primary Immunodeficiency (PI).

  • Complex immune system evaluation
  • IgG Subclass deficiency testing
  • Management of IVIG or subcutaneous immunoglobulin therapies
